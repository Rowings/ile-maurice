The Island in all its glory!

Welcome to the wonderful Mauritius Island. Did you know that the country is offering free visas at the moment? Check if you're elligible here. Having first emerged from the ocean 8 million years ago, this volcanic island has been inhabited by a vast majority of the world's cultures; Arab, Malay, Portuguese, Dutch, French, British before declaring its own independence in 1968. Withs its 177km of coastline covered entirely by bright sunny beaches and coral reefs, you are sure to find your pleasure on this tropical paradise.

For many years now Mauritius has been uninvolved in any kind of armed or political conflict, which explains its lack of military and army. Native home of the Dodo bird, you will find peace and relaxation at the foot of the palm trees.


Boats on Blue Waters

Mauritius is home to some of bluest and most see-through bodies of water around. Thus, boating is a massive activity on the island. Check out where you can rent one here. Boating and sailing is a must-do activity on the island whilst you're visiting. There are a vast amount of islands close to the main island, you therefore need a boat to get to those secluded beaches and beautiful sceneries.
